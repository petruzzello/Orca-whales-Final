About

Many people believe that there is a great divide between humans and other animals; that our possession of language, culture, traditions, and emotions separate us from the other creatures this earth belongs to. In truth, however, the divide is not as grand as humans make it out to be. This problematic way of thinking causes the mistreatment of many animals. The orca entertainment industry is a prime example of humans knowing that the animals captive are intelligent (as they train them for shows), but believing that since there is an inherent divide between mankind and other animals, it’s okay to keep the whales captive.

We decided to dive deeper into orca captivity and explore the problems surrounding it. We listed some problematic past events involving orcas, as well as alternative solutions to keeping orcas, organizations against orca captivity, and some facts surrounding orca intelligence as well as behaviors

Tilikum attacks Keltie Byrne - Blackfish

WARNING: This video contains disturbing content that might not be suitable for all audiences


Tilikum was a male Orca that was captured in 1983 and was bought by Sealand of the Pacific, a Canadian marine amusement park, to serve as a performer. In order to train Tilikum, he was often paired up with another Orca, one that was trained, and was made to perform tasks. Whenever he failed, both Orcas were punished by being deprived of food. This led to hostile interactions between Tilikum and the other Orcas and there have been multiple instances where Tilikum was attacked by his fellow Orcas. Every night, Tilikum and the other whales were rounded into a facility that was dark, cramped, and devoid of stimulation. Trainers often noticed that Tilikuk would sometimes emerge from this facility the next day with new scrapes and bloodied cuts, injuries that were assumed to be caused by the other Orcas. While he was held in a positive regard by many trainers for his well-mannered behavior, on February 20 1991, Keltie Byrne, a college student, trainer, and athletic swimmer, accidentally fell into the water and was dragged by Tilikum into the depths.

Unfortunately, Tilikum resisted the trainers and their efforts to rescue Byrne and her body eventually pulled out of the water. Many speculate that Tilikum’s violent attack was due to the culmination of the psychological and physical abuse he endured, along with the poor and inhumane conditions of his captivity.

The hostility that the trained Orcas showed towards Tilikum demonstrates both their intelligence for being able to correlate their lack of food with Tilikum’s mistakes, and their capacity to harbor grudges and form complex social relationships with one another. Both of these mean that harsh treatment and poor conditions of captivity for Orcas can produce harm and anguish in a way that can and should be compared to the treatment of Humans

Orcas and elephants are smart, social and way too large for captivity

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


Orcas are notorious for being treated badly during captivity. During captivity, Orcas faces problems such as dental, mental and physical problems. Typically Orcas are harmless in the wild, but due to the limited or no access to dental care, Orcas become viscously aggressive. In addition, Orcas must overcome their mental problems. They are intelligent mammals and able to mourn their young. Sadly, they can do nothing to prevent their babies from dying. 67% of Orcas die before the age of 6 because of how they are treated during captivity. This puts tremendous stress onto the parents and is one of the reasons why they are viciously aggressive. Physical problems are also a major problem during captivity. Orcas are huge mammals and most facilities do not have access to tanks that fit them. Instead, more than 4 dozen Orcas are forced to live in tanks that are not even twice the size of them. Therefore, Orcas should not be in captivity because they will suffer during captivity.

Artificial Alternatives

There are many alternatives for usage of animals for entertainment purposes. New alternatives have already started to be developed. Two really effective alternatives are the usage of holograms or going further to even create animatronics that mimic the animals they are meant to portray. There has recently been a creation of an animatronic dolphin by the name of Belle. She was created with the intention of being a method to reduce the usage of many unethical practices to show animals. She is fully programmable and given specific code to perform specific actions. She acts just as a biological dolphin would be doing tricks, interacting with guests and is able to swim in water.

Another method is the use of holographic animal shows. Using holographic animals will use laser beams or even 3d projectors to show off the animals abilities and entertainment and will also attract a new audience that enjoys light shows and animals (Hemi Kim, Animal Entertainment). With these new technologies there is no doubt there could be an animatronic or even a holographic orca created. It would get rid of the need for orcas being used for entertainment as a whole, with a more ethical way of showing off the orca species and how intelligent and friendly the orca is.

Helpful Organizations

There are many organizations that are against the containment of not only orcas, but dolphins and other marine life. By supporting organizations like these, you are helping to advance the campaign against the mistreatment of orcas and other marine species. Peta is campaigning to save the whales and dolphins at SeaWorld. There are many ways to support the cause without spending money such as signing petitions or spreading the word and encouraging others to not support theme parks like Sea World.

Peta thinks that keeping orcas in captivity is cruel as they were denied everything natural to them. They urge people not to buy tickets or support theme parks like SeaWorld in any way. The International Marine Mammal Project is also advocating for the freedom of all whales and dolphins. They say that although many facilities have been shut down in the US and Europe, there are still many being built in Japan, China, the Caribbean, and the Middle East. You can donate towards the cause or sign a petition. Change.org also has a petition you can sign that can help to end the capture, containment, and breeding of orcas at SeaWorld.
